Historical Overview

​ The organization of the Armed Forces in Saudi Arabia led to the establishment of the Army Medicine in Tafif in 1367 AH. (Military Health) In 1371H Prince Mansour Military Hospital in Taif was opened with capacity 36 beds were increased to 100 beds by the end of the year, in 1373 AH Prince Mashaal Hospital was opened outside, and in 1373 AH the Armed Forces Hospital was opened in Riyadh. In 29/12/1392, the Decree was issued by His Royal Highness the Minister of Defence and Aviation and the Inspector General to amend the name of "General Directorate of Medical Services of the Armed Forces". Its services were extended to all armed forces and became budgeted separately as of fiscal year 1394 AH/1395 AH.
